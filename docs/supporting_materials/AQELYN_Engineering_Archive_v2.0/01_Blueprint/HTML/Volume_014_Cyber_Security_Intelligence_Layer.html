PS-ADR-0011
Intelligence as a Platform Capability
Status: Accepted
Decision
Intelligence  shall  be  a  platform-wide  capability  rather  than  an  isolated  feature  of
individual modules.
Rationale
Centralized  correlation  improves  consistency,  reduces  duplication,  and  enables
explainable reasoning across the platform.

One  capability  I  believe  will  become  the  "brain"  of  Project  AQELYN  is  the  Mission
Orchestrator.
Responsibilities include:
•  Selecting the appropriate engines.
•  Scheduling tasks.
•  Managing dependencies.
•  Tracking mission progress.
•  Handling failures.
•  Coordinating verification.
•  Producing the final mission report.
Every scan, audit, investigation, or compliance review becomes a managed mission.
New Core Engine – Evidence Correlation Engine (ECE)
The Evidence Correlation Engine combines evidence from all platform modules.
Rather than evaluating each observation independently, it asks:
•  Do multiple engines support this conclusion?
•  Are there conflicting observations?
•  Has this behavior occurred before?
•  Which evidence is strongest?
Each conclusion is assigned a confidence level based on the quality and consistency of
the evidence.
New Core Engine – Policy Engine
Every organization defines policies.
Examples:
•  All administrator accounts must use MFA.
•  Production systems must support TLS 1.3.
•  Public APIs must require authentication.
•  Endpoints must have disk encryption enabled.
•  Mobile devices must be protected with a screen lock.
The  Policy  Engine  continuously  evaluates  the  environment  against  these  policies  and
reports compliance, deviations, and recommended actions.

Long-Term Vision: AQELYN OS
I want to share one final architectural thought—not for version 1, but as a direction.
If we continue with this architecture, AQELYN could eventually evolve into what I
would call AQELYN OS.
Not  an  operating  system  in  the  traditional  sense,  but  an  operating  system  for
cybersecurity operations.
Its responsibilities would include:
•  Discovering assets.
•  Understanding relationships.
•  Assessing trust.
•  Detecting change.
•  Prioritizing work.
•  Guiding remediation.
•  Verifying outcomes.
•  Preserving evidence.
•  Supporting compliance.
•  Coordinating investigations.
•  Assisting analysts.
Everything revolves around a single source of truth.
One additional principle
As we move from design to implementation, I want us to adopt a strict rule:
If we cannot explain how a feature works, how it will be implemented in Python,
how it will be tested, and how it will be maintained over the next ten years, it
does not become part of the core platform.
That  discipline  will  help  Project  AQELYN  remain  ambitious,  technically  grounded,  and
maintainable—qualities that are essential if the goal is to build a platform that can one
day  be  trusted  by  individuals,  enterprises,  financial  institutions,  and  government
organizations.
